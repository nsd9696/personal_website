Personal Statement

Becoming a person of influence, especially who creates and spreads impactful social value, has always been my life’s mission. During high school, I volunteered biweekly at a local children’s center, teaching students with little educational access. Playing sports together and mentoring them, I felt rewarded but asked myself, “What should I do to help make a better society?”

My first answer was through education, because it aims to cultivate knowledge, instill morals, discover students’ talent, and hone their aptitude so they can pursue their passion and become valuable members of society. This led me to participate in several educational volunteer opportunities throughout college. For example, I tutored math to vocational high school students who decided to find a job instead of continuing their studies. Although they lacked basic mathematical knowledge at first, I could gradually feel their progress over time. My tireless effort eventually kindled the students’ interest in math and later motivated them to pursue college education. Later, one of them visited me in person to express appreciation for my support in expanding his horizon. This heartwarming moment solidified my vision of enhancing society by sharing my knowledge and experience.

Another turning point confirmed my commitment to graft technology to increase educational equalities and widen opportunities. At the Korean Scholars’ Conference for Youth (KSCY), I mentored on essay writing to high school students interested in majoring in education during college level. For our lessons, we used an idea board that was a bit old and had poor usability. I proposed a web-friendly idea board by changing the configuration and adding unique interactive features, which greatly increased students’ performance and learning. By integrating technology, I realized the capability of engineering to enhance students’ educational achievement. This achievement sparked my interest in computer science in further depth with a goal of contributing to education based on my interdisciplinary academic background.

My endeavors toward social value led me to establish a social startup club. While building the team, I could listen to voices concerned with social problems caused by waste, especially since plastic cups and contents were thrown away together. This generated stink at the library or subway stations and created inefficiency in separating waste. People working at such public places expressed difficulties to our team and led us to invent a trash can that divides plastic cups and liquid easily. We installed the trash can at the library and registered it as a patent. It was a meaningful experience where I could contribute directly to solving social problems.

As a student, I have always committed myself to creating a virtuous cycle in society through innovations using technology. The sequence of my journey to find my role, from education to computer science has given me a critical “turning point” in which I also could present a “turning point” to others. Becoming a computer science expert will provide me with unparalleled opportunities and capabilities to generate social good. Thus, I hope to realize my research passion in computer science through graduate school.
